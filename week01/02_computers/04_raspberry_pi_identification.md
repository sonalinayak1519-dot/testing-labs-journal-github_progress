| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| SoC |A SoC (System on a Chip) is a single chip that integrates all the core components of a computer, including the CPU, GPU, memory, and storage, onto one piece of silicon. It is commonly found in smartphones, tablets, and laptops where space and power efficiency are more important than raw performance. |
| GPIO | GPIO (General Purpose Input/Output) are programmable pins found on microcontrollers and single-board computers that can be configured to either send or receive digital signals to and from external devices. They are commonly used in electronics projects to connect and control components such as LEDs, sensors, motors, and buttons.|
| USB | USB (Universal Serial Bus) is a standard interface that allows the computer to connect and communicate with external devices such as keyboards, mice, storage drives, and smartphones. It provides both data transfer and power delivery through a single cable, with newer versions like USB 3.0 and USB-C offering faster speeds and higher power output.|
| HDMI | HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface) is a standard interface that transmits both high-definition video and audio signals through a single cable from a computer or device to a display such as a monitor or TV. Newer versions like HDMI 2.0 and 2.1 support higher resolutions, faster refresh rates, and features like 4K and 8K output.|
| Power | Power in a computer refers to the electrical energy supplied to all components through the power supply unit, which converts AC power from the wall into the DC voltages required by each part of the system. Without a stable and sufficient power supply, the computer will not function correctly, and components can be damaged by insufficient or unstable power delivery.|
| microSD | A microSD card is a tiny removable storage device commonly used in smartphones, tablets, cameras, and single-board computers to expand their storage capacity. It uses flash memory to permanently store data such as photos, videos, and files, and can easily be inserted and removed from a compatible slot.|
| Network |A network interface allows a computer to connect and communicate with other devices and the internet, either through a wired Ethernet connection or wirelessly via Wi-Fi. It handles the sending and receiving of data packets between devices, enabling file sharing, internet browsing, and communication across local and wide area networks. |
| Wireless | Wireless connectivity allows a computer or device to connect to a network or other devices without the need for physical cables, using radio frequency signals such as Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. Wi-Fi enables internet access over a local network while Bluetooth allows short-range communication between devices such as headphones, keyboards, and smartphones.|

## Reflection
Why are many components integrated into a single board in embedded computers such as the Raspberry Pi?
Write **100 words**.
In embedded computers like the Raspberry Pi, many components are integrated onto a single board to keep the device compact, low-cost, and energy efficient. This design makes it practical for use in small projects, portable devices, and educational purposes where space, power consumption, and affordability are more important than high performance.
