# Part 5 – Final Reflection
Write **200–300 words** describing your learning experience during this lab.
Consider:
* What surprised you about computer hardware?
* What components were new to you?
* What challenges did you face?
* What did you learn about embedded systems?
* Honestly, what surprised me most is just how small everything has gotten. It is hard to believe that a tiny chip no bigger than a fingernail can contain billions of transistors and run an entire operating system. I also did not realise how many different components need to work together just to do something as simple as opening a web browser — the CPU, RAM, storage, and motherboard all have to communicate with each other instantly and without errors. It really changes the way you look at computers when you understand what is actually happening inside them.
* Honestly, the GPIO pins were completely new to me. I had never heard of them before and did not expect that a small board could physically control things like lights and sensors through simple pins. The SoC was also new to me — I never thought an entire computer system could fit onto one single chip. It was surprising to learn that devices like the Raspberry Pi have everything built into one small board rather than having separate components like a regular desktop PC.
* Honestly, the biggest challenge for me was the terminal commands. I am used to using a mouse and clicking through folders, so typing commands to navigate and manage files felt quite unfamiliar at first. It was easy to make small mistakes like typing the wrong command or forgetting a space, and then not understanding why it was not working. Understanding the difference between all the different components was also a bit overwhelming at first since there are so many parts and they all have different functions. But once I started practising and things began to make sense, it got a lot easier.
* Honestly, I learned that embedded systems are a lot more capable than I expected. Before this, I thought of computers as just desktops or laptops, so it was interesting to discover that something as small as a Raspberry Pi can function as a fully working computer. I learned that embedded systems are designed to be compact, low power, and affordable, which is why so many components are integrated onto a single board. It was also interesting to learn that they are used in so many everyday devices like smartphones, cameras, and smart home gadgets. It really opened my eyes to how much computing is happening all around us in devices we do not even think of as computers.
